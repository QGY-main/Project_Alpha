01 · What we do

A stigma-free space.

Most Values-in-Action projects focus on underprivileged communities. Project Alpha addresses a different, equally real need: Integrated Programme and O-Level track students who are academically capable but not always getting support in time. Their struggles are rarely visible — which is exactly why they get overlooked.

We run small-group and peer-to-peer mentoring in core STEM subjects, pairing concept reinforcement with exam practice, and stretching stronger students with Olympiad-level enrichment where it's wanted. The goal isn't just better grades — it's rebuilding confidence and keeping students engaged in their own education.

02 · Interschool by design

One project, two schools, shared ownership.

Every week, one school opens its doors and the other dials in — tutors join live over Zoom from whichever campus isn't hosting, matched to students by subject expertise, so a student never loses access to the right specialist just because of where a session happens to be. Hosting itself rotates on a fixed weekly cycle between Catholic High School and St Nicholas Girls' School, so neither school carries the project alone; both are equally home to it.

03 · Objectives

Built to close gaps now, and outlast our own time in secondary school.

Bridge the gap

Close learning gaps opened up by a fast, high-pressure curriculum — before they compound.

Remove the stigma

A safe environment where asking for help isn't a mark against you.

Grow our mentors

Turn student volunteers into empathetic, subject-competent educators.

Cover core STEM

Structured support across Add Maths, E Maths, Physics, Chemistry and Biology.

Scale into EJC

Both partner schools feed into Eunoia Junior College — Project Alpha is designed to follow.

Protect our mentors

Rotation and shift systems, plus regular exco check-ins, guard against burnout.
